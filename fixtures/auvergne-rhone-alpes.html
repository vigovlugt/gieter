Major sites in Auvergne-Rhône-Alpes

  • Le Bugey

    From the banks of the Rhône to the Retord and Valromey plateaus, the Bugey region has a thousand wonderful surprises in store for you. Whether you're a hiker, a walker, a cyclist, a skier, a fisherman or a swimmer, the beauty, diversity and treasures of Bugey's natural environment will win you over.

  • The Saint-Pourçain Vineyard

    The Allier region has its own Saint-Pourçain wine. A unique grape variety: tressallier! The Vineyard of Saint-Pourçain is said to be older than the Romans! 19 communes in the Bourbonnais region produce red, white and rosé wines classified as Appellation d'Origine Contrôlée.

  • The Ardèche gorges

    32 kilometers of limestone gorges between Vallon-Pont-d'Arc and Saint-Martin-d'Ardèche await water sports enthusiasts. In this exceptional natural site, classified as a National Nature Reserve, man and nature live in harmony.

  • Lake Saint Etienne Cantales

    The Lake de Saint-Étienne-Cantalès is located in the heart of the rolling, wooded landscape of the Cantal chestnut region. It has a highly indented shape and numerous peninsulas. It is an artificial lake.

  • The perched villages of the Drôme

    Dive into the Drôme Provençale! The Drôme region bridges the gap between the lavender fields of Provence and the mountain massifs. Take the road to its hilltop villages, and you'll be fascinated by their authenticity and beauty!

  • Alpe d'huez in Isère

    The Alpe d'Huez ski resort is located in the Oisans region, the land of rock and altitude. You'll find yourself surrounded by some of the most grandiose scenery in the world, with the nearby Meije at 3,984m and the Romanche valley virtually untouched by human intervention.

  • The Polignac fortress

    As soon as you arrive in the basin of Le Puy en Velay, you'll see the keep of the fortress standing proudly in the distance. Between November 9 and 11, you can attend a majestic event, "the annual gathering of the Montgolfières du Velay"

  • The Chaine des Puys

    france's 1st UNESCO World Heritage Site and 4th in France, the Chaîne des Puys-Faille de Limagne is now one of the 44 French sites listed.

  • Condrieu

    On steep hillsides, the prestigious vineyards of Côte-Rôtie and Condrieu dominate the Rhône River. Enjoy these delicate wines with Rigottes de Condrieu, a small raw-milk goat's cheese. On the other side of the Rhône, neighboring Vienne will enchant you with its historical and cultural heritage, and take you on a journey through the great periods of French history.

  • Courchevel and Méribel

    The Col de la Loze, the link between the Courchevel and Méribel valleys, is one of the most beautiful peaks in the 3 Vallées! At 2274 metres, the summit offers breathtaking panoramic views!

  • Lake Geneva

    Located in the north of the Haute-Savoie department, Lake Geneva is the largest lake in Western Europe. A true link between Switzerland and France, Lake Geneva stretches from Geneva to Montreux, passing through the cities of Lausanne and Vevey.

  • The Côte roannaise

    An invitation to travel back in time through the five villages of Roannais character: Ambierle, le Crozet, Saint Haon, le Châtel, St Jean St Maurice sur Loire and Charlieu.

Discover the Auvergne-Rhône-Alpes region in all its colors

Auvergne-Rhône-Alpes, born of the merger of Rhône-Alpes and Auvergne, is a region characterized by its geographical diversity, including mountain peaks, verdant valleys and culturally rich cities. From gastronomy to historic sites and outdoor activities, it offers visitors an unforgettable experience.

From the Forest du Tronçais to the summits of the Puys, theAuvergne offers you a wealth of wonders within easy reach: farmland and forests dotted with castles, medieval towns, absolutely wild spaces and curious volcanic reliefs, including the spectacular panorama of the Chaîne des Puys over an alignment of eighty wonderfully preserved volcanoes...
Follow in the footsteps of Vercingetorix or Queen Margot on the Route des Châteaux, from austere medieval fortresses to the elegant residences of the Dukes of Bourbon. Discover Romanesque masterpieces by visiting the churches of Saint Nectaire, Orcival or Issoire. Whether you're an adventurer or a philosophical angler, you'll find it easy to enjoy your favorite pastimes: Hiking or mountain biking, canoeing and rafting on the Allier, and, in winter, skiing on the snow-covered slopes of the Sancy or Plomb du Cantal. Water will be everywhere in your path, on your walks or in one of the region's ten 'water cities'.

The Rhône-Alpes region offers a wide range of landscapes. The wet Dombes region spreads its thousand ponds to the outskirts of Lyon. To the west, the Vineyards of the Rhône Valley are world-renowned, while the east boasts Mont-Blanc. The south also has a lot to offer: the gorges of the Ardèche, the high plateaus of the Vercors and the lavender fields of the Drôme. On the slopes of the Alps, more than 6,000 km of pistes await you on the world's largest ski area; in summer, the mountains reveal their assets for other activities: touring the Vanoise lakes, Hiking to Mont Aiguille, walking in the heart of the valleys.
Visit a region of art and history: prehistoric caves, the medieval castle of the Dukes of Savoy in Chambéry, the Cistercian abbey of Aiguebelle in the Drôme, the Renaissance district of Lyon... Your escapades will be the perfect excuse to sample one of the region's specialties: gratin dauphinois, raclette or fondue, Romans ravioli, hot sausage or tripe accompanied by a jug of Beaujolais or Côtes du Rhône.

Map of Auvergne-Rhône-Alpes

From the Jura mountains to the expanses of the Dombes and the vineyards of Bugey, theAin region offers a multitude of landscapes to discover. For nature lovers, the Haut-Jura Natural Park offers exceptional panoramic views on foot or by bike. 
The Allier region is characterized by its generous, unspoilt nature. The Tronçais Forest, Europe's largest oak forest, is ideal for hiking, mountain biking and horseback riding. The Sioule valley offers an enchanting setting for canoeing, fishing and river walks.
The Ardèche offers a wide variety of landscapes and sumptuous panoramas. It's an ideal playground for all kinds of outdoor activities. From the Ardèche gorges, popular for canoeing and kayaking, to the Ardèche mountains, perfect for hiking and mountain biking, and countless rivers and waterfalls for swimming.
Cantal is famous for its peaks and mountains, part of the majestic Massif Central. Puy Mary, Plomb du Cantal and the Parc naturel régional des Volcans d'Auvergne offer breathtaking panoramas. Cantal's rich flora and fauna also make it a paradise for hikers, mountain bikers and nature lovers.

The Drôme will seduce you with its landscapes, historical treasures and gourmet specialties. From the Vercors Regional Nature Park to the foothills of the Provençal Baronnies, via the lavender and olive groves of the Montélimar plain, the panoramas are varied and rejuvenating.
The Haute-Loire, with its verdant landscapes, volcanoes, Velay plateaux and wild gorges, is also rich in historical heritage, including the famous Puy-en-Velay, with its Notre-Dame cathedral and statue of Notre-Dame de France, the fortified castle of Polignac, the fortress of Saint-Vidal and the medieval town of Brioude.
From its high mountains to its vast plains, from the Vercors Natural Park to the steep ridges of the Oisans and the Chartreuse,Isère offers sumptuous landscapes perfect for Hiking, mountain biking, climbing and, in winter, skiing and snowboarding.
The Loire is a land of contrasts, from the verdant Pilat mountains to the Forez plain. Stroll through the vineyards, hike or bike along the many trails. In winter, the Monts du Pilat are ideal for Alpine skiing, Cross-country skiing, snowshoeing and tobogganing.
The Puy-de-Dôme takes its name from the volcano that dominates the region, a sleeping giant whose summit offers an exceptional panoramic view of the Chaîne des Puys, a chain of 80 volcanoes! It's an ideal playground for hiking, mountain biking and horseback riding.
The Rhône offers a surprising variety of landscapes. To the east, the metropolis of Lyon lies at the confluence of the Saône and Rhône rivers, while to the west, the mountains of Beaujolais and Lyonnais offer a verdant setting. You'll discover many historical and cultural sites ranging from antiquity to the present day.
Savoie is a paradise for lovers of mountains and breathtaking panoramas. From Mont Blanc, Europe's highest peak, to the Vanoise, Chartreuse and Bauges massifs, Savoie is home to a variety of sumptuous landscapes. 
Haute-Savoie stretches from the majestic Mont Blanc, the roof of Europe, to the Aravis, Chablais and Bornes massifs. A region of skiing and mountain activities, it is also renowned for its lakes Annecy, Léman, Passy and Montriond, ideal for swimming and water sports.
